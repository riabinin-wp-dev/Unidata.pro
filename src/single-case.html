Liveness Detection

Anti-Spoofing for a biometric access system reduced the number of missed attacks from 18% to 1%
Industry and use case:
Safety
Data:
10,000 video attacks
Project duration:
21 days

Our Challenge:

The client required an enhancement in the metrics of their biometric access required an enhancement in the metrics of their biometric enhancement in the metrics of their biometric access required an enhancement in the metrics of their biometric access system for banking programs; the neural network was missing 18% of attacks. According to specifications, the anti-spoofing dataset necessitated a diverse array of mobile devices and accessories (masks, wigs, glasses, scarves, etc.). The client approached us due to their internal team’s unsuccessful experience in data collection, coupled with time constraints regarding the dataset

Our Solution:

Since the biometric system only required refinement for complex and atypical appearance scenarios, we artificially generated 10,000 video attacks. We assembled a team of 20 individuals who created diverse filming conditions, using paper masks and various props. Each assessor simulated attacks involving 500 individuals

Optimizing Harvest Efficiency

A client in the agriculture industry approached Unidata to automate the strawberry-picking process and increase farm efficiency. With cameras capturing close-up images of the strawberries, the task was to classify them by ripeness to streamline harvesting.

Industry:
Agriculture
Data:
5,000 strawberry images
Timeline:
1 month
Industry:

Data:

Objective:

The strawberry producer approached Unidata to automate their berry-picking process and improve farm efficiency. Cameras installed on the farm captured close-up images of strawberries, and the task was to classify the strawberries based on ripeness to optimize the picking process.

Solution:

  • 01

    Requirements and Pilot Project:

    The client provided strawberry images and classification instructions to categorize berries into three groups: ripe, unripe, and partially ripe. We reviewed these requirements and suggested refinements to the classification criteria to make the annotation process as accurate and user-friendly as possible.
